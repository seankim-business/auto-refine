What exactly gets optimized?

auto-refine does not optimize the whole world. It optimizes a bounded artifact against a trusted evaluator, under explicit constraints, with rollback when a candidate is worse or unsafe.

What the engine actually decides

The useful part is not “run some experiments.” The useful part is the decision boundary: what gets kept, what gets rejected, and how the incumbent remains clean.

Proof examples that show the mechanism clearly

These are deterministic local proof examples, not production benchmark claims. They are here to show the product behavior legibly: the artifact changes, the evaluator scores it, constraints block bad candidates, and the incumbent only advances on accepted improvements.
